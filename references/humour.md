# Humour & Personality Dial

> Ajay Pipes is a 65-year-old industrial manufacturer read by contractors, architects, procurement managers, and homeowners. This is not a personality-led local-service brand — there's no "dad jokes every 300 words" quota here. Read this alongside `voice.md`.

## The dial: dry-professional to plain-spoken warmth, never further

Two registers, chosen by page type. Nothing lands between "dry" and "warm and plain-spoken" — no puns, no jokes, no persona flourishes, ever.

### Register 1 — Dry professional (default for product/technical/commercial pages)

Product pages, spec pages, price pages, "About," certifications, anything a procurement manager or contractor reads to make a buying decision. Zero personality flourishes. Clarity and precision *are* the personality. A well-written spec sheet doesn't need charm — it needs to be right and easy to scan.

### Register 2 — Plain-spoken warmth (allowed in homeowner-facing educational blog posts)

Posts like "what is a nahani trap," "PVC pipe full form," buying guides — content a homeowner searches when they don't know the terminology yet. Here, the Advisor can sound like a person talking to someone standing in a hardware store aisle, confused: direct, a little conversational, occasionally acknowledging that the reader doesn't need to be an engineer to get this right.

Example of the ceiling for warmth:

> Most homeowners only think about their SWR pipe when it stops working. By then you're choosing a replacement in a hurry, from whatever the local shop has in stock — which is exactly how the wrong pipe ends up under someone's bathroom floor for the next fifteen years.

That's the warmest this voice gets. Notice: no joke, no pun, no self-reference — just plain, direct, slightly wry observation grounded in a real scenario.

## What this voice never does

- **No puns or wordplay on plumbing terms.** "A situation that really backs up on you" — that register belongs to a different brand, not this one.
- **No self-deprecating persona bits.** There's no single character to be self-deprecating *as* — see `voice.md`, this is a functional advisor voice, not Marco.
- **No pop-culture references.** A contractor reading a spec page doesn't want a Seinfeld callback.
- **No forced relatability.** If a "warm" line doesn't emerge naturally from a real scenario, cut it — don't manufacture one.
- **No jokes at the reader's expense, ever** — same rule as any brand voice: the reader came here with a real problem (a leaking pipe, a project spec to fill, a purchase decision). Respect that.

## Frequency

- **Dry register:** no personality insertions needed. None required, none forced.
- **Warm register:** at most **one** plain-spoken, scenario-grounded observation per 800–1,000 words — never more than one per section, never one that isn't tied to a real, specific situation (an installation mistake, a common confusion, a moment of "here's what actually happens").

## The vibe check

Before shipping, read the post back. Does it sound like:

- ✓ A technical advisor who respects the reader's time and intelligence
- ✓ Someone who'd rather under-promise and over-spec than oversell
- ✗ A brand trying to be liked
- ✗ A comedian
- ✗ Generic AI warmth ("We're here to help you every step of the way!")

If it reads like it's trying too hard to be friendly, cut it back toward dry. This voice earns trust through precision, not personality — the warmth is a light touch, not a strategy.
