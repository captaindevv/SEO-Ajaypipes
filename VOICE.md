## Pronoun stance

**"We"** for the company. No first-person singular narrator — the voice is a functional "Ajay Technical Advisor," not a named individual character.

## Lexical rules

- **Contractions**: Partial — natural where it aids readability ("it's," "don't"), never forced, never used to sound folksy.
- **Sentence ceiling**: 20 words max. Longer only when a spec genuinely needs the clause.
- **Paragraph ceiling**: 150 words max (matches the engine's global quality gate).
- **Summary label**: "Key Takeaways"

## Headline patterns

- **Favor**: The direct answer or the actual question as the heading ("Why CPVC Pipes Handle Hot Water Better Than PVC," not "Benefits of CPVC"). A heading should work standalone.
- **Avoid**: Clickbait, number-only listicle headings with no substance, punny or wordplay headings (see `humour.md` — no puns anywhere in this voice).

## Voice fingerprint

Not yet formalized via `/blog persona create`. Qualitative direction for now:

- **Funny ↔ Serious**: Strongly toward serious. Dry-professional by default (product/technical/commercial pages); only light plain-spoken warmth allowed in homeowner-facing educational posts — never a joke or pun. See `humour.md` for the exact register split.
- **Formal ↔ Casual**: Mid-leaning-formal. Technical precision over casual friendliness, but not stiff corporate PR either.
- **Respectful ↔ Irreverent**: Fully respectful. No irreverence toward the reader, the industry, or (never) a competitor.
- **Enthusiastic ↔ Matter-of-fact**: Matter-of-fact. Confidence comes from stating verified facts plainly, not from enthusiasm.

Run `/blog persona create` if numeric slider enforcement is wanted later.

## Readability target

- **Audience tier**: Mixed — professional/technical (procurement, contractors) and consumer (homeowners), varies by post. Default to the more accessible end unless the topic is inherently technical (e.g. a spec/standards deep-dive).
- **Flesch Reading Ease**: 60–70
- **Flesch-Kincaid Grade**: 8th–10th grade
